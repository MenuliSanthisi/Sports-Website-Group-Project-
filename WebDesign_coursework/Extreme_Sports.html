Extreme Sports

"Better to have a short life
that is full of what you love doing,
than a long life spent in a miserable way"

Extreme sports are all about the thrill.
For some people its about pure fun, and for others its about testing the limits of what is humanly possible.
To whatever degree,extremes sports all carry a certain wow factor, giving them a unique sense of appeal.
Activities categorized by media as extreme sports differ from traditional sports due to the higher number of inherently uncontrollable variables.
These environmental variables are frequently weather and terrain related, including wind, snow, water and mountains.
Because these natural phenomena cannot be controlled, they inevitably affect the outcome of the given activity or event.
Here is a list of extreme sports, listed from 'INTENSE' to outright 'INSANE'!

1. SCUBA DIVING

Some might not feel scuba diving is not an extreme sport.But when we think of yourself 100+ feet underwater and the only thing that keeps you alive is a tank of oxygen strapped to your back. There will typically be an element that we cannot control such as the natural environment which is ever-changing and indifferent. For example, when scuba diving it is difficult to predict the flow of the currents, the behavior of any aquatic life that is encountered, or the reliability of the SCUBA gear that can suddenly fail for one reason or another. It is also hard to predict how one’s body reacts to nitrogen narcosis at a deep enough depth.

2. SKY DIVING

One of the oldest of all extreme sports. This is originated 200 years ago. This sport invloved into jumping from aircraft and doing a varity of midair tricks, before deploying the parachute. It’s a true adventure–with true risk!–and it requires your full attention and participation. This is no thrill ride. This is no set-it-and-forget-it tilt-o-whirl that you can phone in while you take a selfie. This is no fake. This is the real thing: you, experiencing your unique and precious human experience like a boss.

3. MOUNTAIN BOARDING

Also known as offroad boarding and dirtboarding,This takes all type of terrain, and are sometimes used to do tricks or even downhill races. Riders take huge jumps, carve rails and pull mind-blowing aerial maneuvers in terrain parks and skate parks. For boarder-cross a hybrid of downhill and motocross four riders jostle for position as they tear through banked turns and fly over jumps and drops. Technically it's illegal to knock each other over, but the wrecks are half the fun.

4. PARASAILING

Many probably think this is relaxing activity at the beach. But here you are being pulled up by a piece of rope upto a hundred feet above the water. The harness attaches the pilot to the parasail, which is connected to the boat, or land vehicle, by the tow rope. The vehicle then drives off, carrying the parascender and person into the air. Up to three people can parasail behind it at the same time. It’s also one of the coolest things to do in Key West!

5. WHITE WATER RAFTING

Using an inflatable raft or kayak on an unstable, tubulent current flowing white water. Rafter risks hitting sharp rocks, unexpected steep waterfalls and being thrown from the raft.Rafting is also a competitive sport practiced around the world which culminates in a world rafting championship event between the participating nations. The International Rafting Federation, often referred to as the IRF, is the worldwide body which oversees all aspects of the sport.
